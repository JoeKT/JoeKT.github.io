Game Algorithm: Economics

Players required to manage complex, multi-variate economics were faced with an unreasonable knowledge barrier and could not focus on the actual gameplay. By reducing economies to a solitary value without worrying about differing currencies, this problem was alleviated.

Below are the formulas from which the economic values are derived.


Economic Formula (Version 1)

Economic Points = { ( [ National Stability / 2 ] * [Industrial Development - Devestation ] * [ # of Cities + ( # of Trade Routes / 10 ) ] * (Total Value of Goods Produced) ] ) }

This formula produced a ratio of 1:358 between the highest and lowest economies. It produced an average income of 768.797, which is higher than the income for 78.409% of the nations.


Economic Formula (Version 2)

Economic Points = ( { [# of Cities / 10 + 1 ] + [ Industrial Development - Devestation ] + [ # of Trade Routes / 10 + 1 ] + National Stability / 10 + 1 } * Total Value of Goods Produced ) * ( 1 + National Authority / 10 )

This formula produced a ratio of 1:26.930 between the highest and lowest economies. It produced an average income of 337.459, which is higher than the income for 60.227% of the nations. While still a large range and ratio between extremes, the average nation gained significantly and the extremely high performers were brought down to a playable level.
