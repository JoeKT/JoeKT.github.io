The purpose of this sketch was to test the interaction of acrylic paint, pastels, charcoal, and ink for compatibility.

Perhaps my favorite medium, pen and ink allow me to focus on the details of a piece to a degree like no other. Such detail-oriented work requires a clear understanding of the design and requires a firm grasp of different line types, weights, and textures.

Learning what tools and techniques to use for different effects is an important part of learning to design. When I was required to create an acrylic self-portrait, I turned to paint sticks. Still acrylic and allowing for the blending techniques of paint, these sticks also allowed me to utilize my dry media skill sets.

For this acrylic self-portrait I chose to paint with a palette knife instead of a brush; the bold, sharp strokes increased the emotional feel of the piece while providing a level of abstraction that complimented the unusual face I was making.

This plaster replica of my arm was built atop a mesh wire frame before being detailed with pastel, solder, and denim. Training in 3-dimensional art helped foster my understanding of spatial relationships in design and expanded my studies of the interplay between positive/negative space.
