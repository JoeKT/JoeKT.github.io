Theatrical
Design

An understanding of scope and space are incredibly important across all aspects of design. This drafting, created with a perspective grid, this type of drawing allows for a glimpse of the final product before committing to the more intensive parts of the design process.

After research, blocking (traffic flow analysis for the actors) and quick scenery sketches were completed to rough out ideas. The best of these ideas were compiled and drafted with perfect precision to 1/4" scale to create a detailed map for design production.

Every piece of scenery and furniture was drafted in 1/4" scale, as well as the theatre housing the show. This white model displays exactly what an audience member will see. Scenes may be played out to help fine-tune the design and then everything enters full-scale production.

I was both the scenic and projection designer for Marisol. The small space required simple designs that could have very different looks with quick adjustments and the short distance from the audience created the need for an unusually high level of detail. My designs came in 85% under budget and ahead of schedule.
