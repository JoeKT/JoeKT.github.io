Team Hydrogen Trioxide requested a design that reflected their name. I asked for clarification from the team members and given the descriptors "blue" and "swirly". The next day I presented a set of sketches encompassing a wide range of "blue/swirly" designs and then refined several.

I needed a geometric emblem and so headed down to the library for research material. After sketching motifs from a variety of styles, the top left of these emblems caught the client's attention. I simplified the emblem and implemented it in the over-all design.

A contract for a publishing company required the creation of cover art for an anthology collection. I created 4 first-round mock-ups to showcase different ideas and gather feedback. After which, higher-fidelity mock-up were created with cleaner lines.

This was a third-round, high-fidelity mock-up. This layout had been selected by the client and this was one of ten high-fidelity mock-ups with different texturing and unique color schemes.

A sketch done while listening to the owner of Red Letter Publishing talk about his company.
