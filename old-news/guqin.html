Guqin: The Iconic Chinese Sound

The guqin, sometimes simply called qin, translates to "ancient stringed instrument". Its origin lies around the year 3000BC when it was created by Fuxi, Shennong, and The Yellow Emperor.

Held in high regard as a pinnacle of musical culture in China, the guqin is most often associated with scholars, sages, and literati. It is often played at the royal court and in ritual contexts.

The guqin is a five silk-stringed instrument with a long body that sits across the lap. Its notes are soft and span a short range of four octaves. The music played is often slow and serene with its roots in the natural world.

Guqin-players are frequently the subject of traditional arts and referenced in literature.
