Online Game Platform: Europe1477

Case Study Overview

Designed for players with a passion for history and creative storytelling, each player guides a nation through the ages with storytelling, creativity, and strategy.

The Project

Similar games exist but are run though, and structured by, modifications to existing video games or in forum settings. This leads to games struggling to attract players and ending prematurely. The goal of Europe1477 is to create a codified set of rules and algorithms to decrease the workload on moderators while providing a stable and friendly platform with the possiblity of expansion to allow custom-created games.

Personas

Persona One: John Mendez

Archetype: Unengaged high school student

John is a high school student in some more advanced classes with average to slightly above average grades. He does no particularly care about school and find history class especially boring.

Europe1477 was a class requirement and seemed a bit complicated at first because he did not want to put effort into using it. However, it does allow him to use his creative writing skills--a passion he never thought compatible with history.

Doing research for the game stories has increased his understanding of history without being as dull as his text book. He is even noticing it is easier to do well on the class exams.

Must Haves:

  • Easy to follow gameplay
  • Not punishing to players not interested in investing heavily/using advanced features
  • Restricted game options (eg, specific class, school, etc.)

Persona Two: Keone Maru

Archetype: Avid strategy and alt-history gamer

Keone participated in Model UN but never found it indepth enough. He plays Axis and Allies for the detail and enjoys imagining how different WWII outcomes could have shapped the world. He finds the board and rules constricting at times though.

He has participated in similar story-driven history games before on various forums but is frustrated by the lack of players and the necessity of following multiple threads and the sub-optimal constraints on graphics.

A dedicated platform such as Europe1477 would increase visibility since the game would no longer be buried in a forum. The ability to track all a game's data in one place would cut down on the time cost and allow him to create more content in game.

Must Haves:

  • High level of customization both as a moderator and a player
  • Allow for lots of information to be added and easily accessed
  • Tracking/rating system, allowing good players (reliable, fun, involved) to be recognized across games

Designing Europe 1477

    Designing the game:

  • Testing of similar existing game concepts
  • In-depth research of the political, historical, socio-economic, religious, industrial, military, and cultural climate of late Medeival and Renaissance Europe
  • Writing and testing algorithms to strike the balance between realism and playability
  • Allowing player freedom of choice to shape the game
  • Creating structures to reduce moderator workload without limiting players
  • Compiling statistics for each nation and constructing the map
  • ALPHA test
  • Refining gameplay

    Designing the game platform:

  • BETA test for player painpoints
  • Sketch layout designs for a platform that encourages communication and player creation
  • Analyize and modify designs to ensure platform is robust enough for players who are more involved while not penalizing more casual players
  • Identify basic components: player interface, contacts and chat features, game searching and hosting, templature, and publication / update pushpin system
  • Prototype platform with Photoshop and InvisionApp
  • Identify secondary components for prototype expansion: moderator calculators and interface, ability to customize starting national statistics and map, ability to customize templates available to players
